আৰম্ভ কৰক

আমাৰ লক্ষ্য

"Raising Strong Daughters" হৈছে অসমৰ মহিলা আৰু জীয়ৰীসকলৰ বাবে এক আশাৰ বন্তি। আমি জ্ঞান আৰু অনুপ্ৰেৰণাৰ এক ডিজিটেল আশ্ৰয়স্থল গঢ়ি তুলিছোঁ—য'ত অধিকাৰ আৰু স্বাস্থ্যৰ গুৰুত্বপূৰ্ণ তথ্যৰ লগতে আমাৰ নিজৰ সমাজৰ শক্তিশালী কাহিনীসমূহো উপলব্ধ। চাহ বাগিচাৰ পৰা আৰম্ভ কৰি প্ৰযুক্তি জগতলৈকে সকলোৰে কণ্ঠক সংযোগ কৰি আমি মৌনতা ভংগ কৰিবলৈ আৰু সহায়ৰ সাঁকো গঢ়িবলৈ প্ৰচেষ্টা চলাইছোঁ। আমাৰ লক্ষ্য সৰল অথচ গভীৰ: এখন এনে সমাজ গঢ়া য'ত লিংগ সমতা এক স্বাভাৱিক নিয়ম, আৰু প্ৰতিগৰাকী ছোৱালীৰ হাতত নিজৰ ভাগ্য গঢ়াৰ আহিলা থাকে। আহক, আমি সজাগথাক কৰ্মলৈ ৰূপান্তৰিত কৰোঁ আৰু সকলোৰে বাবে আশা, মৰ্যাদা আৰু অসীম সম্ভাৱনাৰে ভৰা এক ভৱিষ্যত সৃষ্টি কৰোঁ।

মোৰ প্ৰেৰণা

Professor Rituparna Patgiri

এই প্ৰকল্পটো মোৰ বাবে অত্যন্ত ব্যক্তিগত, যিটো গঢ় লৈ উঠিছে মোৰ গুৰু, আই.আই.টি. গুৱাহাটীৰ মানৱীয় আৰু সমাজ বিজ্ঞান বিভাগৰ অধ্যাপক ৰিতুপৰ্ণা পাটগিৰিৰ গভীৰ প্ৰভাৱৰ ফলত। 'লিংগ, মিডিয়া, আৰু ডিজিটেল সমাজ'ৰ ওপৰত তেখেতৰ অগ্ৰণী গৱেষণা মোৰ বাবে কেৱল এক শৈক্ষিক তথ্য নহয়; ই বিশ্বক চোৱাৰ মোৰ দৃষ্টিভংগী সলনি কৰি দিছে।

"তেখেতৰ তত্ত্বাৱধানত কাম কৰি মই উপলব্ধি কৰিলোঁ যে প্ৰযুক্তি কেৱল কোড বা সংযোগৰ বিষয় নহয়—ই সামাজিক পৰিৱৰ্তনৰ এক গুৰুত্বপূৰ্ণ স্থান। অধ্যাপক পাটগিৰিৰ অন্তৰ্দৃষ্টিয়ে মোক দেখুৱাই দিছিল যে ডিজিটেল প্লেটফৰ্মবোৰে কেনেকৈ সামাজিক পৰিৱৰ্তনৰ শক্তিশালী ইঞ্জিন হ'ব পাৰে। তেখেতৰ কামেই মোক এই কথা বুজাই দিছিল যে এখন ৱেবছাইট কেৱল তথ্যতকৈও অধিক হ'ব পাৰে; ই অসমত লিংগ সজাগতাৰ বাবে এক সৱলীকৰণৰ আহিলা হ'ব পাৰে।"

এই প্ৰকল্পটো তেখেতৰ নিৰ্দেশনাৰ এক প্ৰমাণ, যিয়ে মোক ইঞ্জিনিয়াৰিংৰ কাৰিকৰী দিশৰ বাহিৰলৈ গৈ স্ক্ৰীণৰ আঁৰৰ মানৱীয় কাহিনীোৰক চাবলৈ শিকাইছে। আমাৰ ডিজিটেল যুগত লিংগৰ সূক্ষ্মতা বুজিবলৈ তেখেতৰ নিষ্ঠাই মোক 'Raising Strong Daughters' গঢ়িবলৈ অনুপ্ৰাণিত কৰিছে—কেৱল এক প্ৰকল্প হিচাপে নহয়, বৰঞ্চ তেখেতে কল্পনা কৰা সমতাপূৰ্ণ সমাজখনলৈ বৰঙণি আগবঢ়োৱাৰ এক আন্তৰিক প্ৰচেষ্টা হিচাপে।

শিক্ষা আৰু সজাগতা প'ৰ্টেল

লিংগ সমতা মানে কেৱল মহিলাৰ অধিকাৰ নহয়, ই পুৰুষ আৰু মহিলা উভয়েই সমান সুযোগ, অধিকাৰ আৰু দায়িত্ব পোৱাটো নিশ্চিত কৰে। ভাৰতীয় সংবিধানৰ অনুচ্ছেদ ১৪, ১৫, আৰু ২১-এ সমতা আৰু মৰ্যাদাৰ অধিকাৰ প্ৰদান কৰে। এই শিতানত, আমি এই অধিকাৰসমূহ আৰু দৈনন্দিন জীৱনত ইয়াৰ প্ৰয়োগৰ বিষয়ে আলোচনা কৰিম।

এগৰাকী শিক্ষিত আৰু স্বাস্থ্যৱান ছোৱালীয়েই এখন সুস্থ সমাজৰ ভেটি। এই শিতানত মাহেকীয়া স্বাস্থ্য (menstrual hygiene), পুষ্টি, আৰু মানসিক স্বাস্থ্যৰ গুৰুত্ব সম্পৰ্কে আলোচনা কৰা হৈছে। শিক্ষাৰ জৰিয়তে ছোৱালী এজনী কেনেদৰে স্বাৱলম্বী হ'b পাৰে, তাৰ বিষয়েও ইয়াত তথ্য দিয়া হৈছে।

মিডিয়া আৰু কলাই সমাজৰ ধাৰণা গঢ়ি তোলে। এই শিতানত আমি অসমৰ মহিলাসকলে কেনেদৰে কলা, সাহিত্য, আৰু মিডিয়াত অৱদান আগবঢ়াইছে, সেই বিষয়ে জানিম। ইয়াৰ উপৰিও, মহিলা উদ্যোগীসকলৰ বাবে থকা সুযোগ আৰু তেওঁলোকৰ সফলতাক আমি উদযাপন কৰিম।

Shaheed Bhogeswari Phukanani: The Lioness of Assam's 1942 Quit India Movement

Shaheed Bhogeswari Phukanani (1885 - 1942)

In the grand saga of India's freedom struggle, the names of prominent leaders are well-known. However, the foundation of this independence was built on the sacrifices of countless unsung heroes—ordinary men and women who displayed extraordinary courage. Among these revered figures is Shaheed Bhogeswari Phukanani, a braveheart from Assam who, at over 60 years of age, laid down her life for the nation's honour.

Her story is a powerful testament to the fact that patriotism and the courage to fight oppression are not bound by age.

The Call of a Nation

Born in 1885 in the Nagaon district of Assam, Bhogeswari Phukanani was a simple housewife and a mother to eight children. Despite her domestic life, she was deeply imbued with the spirit of nationalism. She and her family were active participants in the freedom movement, inspired by Mahatma Gandhi's call for self-reliance and non-cooperation.

When the Quit India Movement was launched in August 1942, it ignited a fire across the nation, demanding the British "Quit India." In Assam, the movement was particularly intense. The British Raj responded with brutal force, banning the Indian National Congress, seizing its offices, and arresting its leaders.

In the Berhampur area of Nagaon, the local Congress office, which Bhogeswari Phukanani had helped establish, was seized and locked by the British authorities. This act was seen as a direct challenge to the local people's pride and their fight for freedom.

The Fateful Day of Defiance

On September 18, 1942, the local community decided to reclaim their office. A massive, peaceful procession was organized to march to the Berhampur Congress office and re-open it.

Bhogeswari Phukanani, then around 60 years old, was at the forefront of this procession. She, along with her daughter Ratnamala, led the crowd, holding the Tiranga (the Indian tricolour flag) high and chanting slogans of "Vande Mataram."

The procession was soon confronted by a large police force led by a British officer, Captain Finish (also referred to as Finch in some accounts). The officer ordered the unarmed crowd to disperse, but they stood their ground.

Enraged by the crowd's defiance, Captain Finish attempted to snatch the national flag from Ratnamala's hands, disrespecting the symbol of their struggle. Seeing this, Bhogeswari Phukanani could not contain her anger. In a powerful, instinctive act of defiance, she took the pole of her own flag and struck Captain Finish on the head.

Stunned and infuriated by the counter-attack from an elderly woman, Captain Finish immediately pulled out his revolver and shot her at point-blank range.

Grievously wounded, Bhogeswari Phukanani fell to the ground. She succumbed to her injuries two days later, on September 20, 1942, achieving martyrdom for the cause of India's independence.

A Legacy That Endures

The sacrifice of Bhogeswari Phukanani did not go in vain. Her martyrdom turned her into a powerful symbol of resistance and courage across Assam. Her act of defending the national flag with her life inspired thousands more to join the Quit India Movement, further strengthening the resolve to oust the British.

Today, Bhogeswari Phukanani is revered as "Shaheed" (Martyr) and celebrated as one of Assam's greatest freedom fighters.

Her legacy is formally remembered in several ways:

  • Bhogeswari Phukanani Civil Hospital: The civil hospital in Nagaon is named in her honour.
  • Bhogeswari Phukanani Indoor Stadium: An indoor stadium in Guwahati is also dedicated to her memory.
  • Commemorative Stamps: India Post has released special covers honouring her as an "Unsung Hero" of the freedom struggle.

Shaheed Bhogeswari Phukanani's story is a vital chapter in India's history. It serves as a profound reminder that the fight for freedom was won by ordinary people who, when the moment came, showed extraordinary courage and were willing to make the ultimate sacrifice for their motherland.

Birbala Kanaklata Barua: The 17-Year-Old Martyr of the Quit India Movement

Birbala Kanaklata Barua (1924 - 1942)

In the annals of India's freedom struggle, there are stories of sacrifice so profound they transcend time. The story of Kanaklata Barua, a young girl from Assam who stared death in the face for the love of her nation, is one of the most powerful and inspiring. At an age when most are just beginning to discover the world, she made the ultimate sacrifice, cementing her legacy as "Birbala" (Brave Girl) and a true martyr.

A Spark Ignited in Assam

Kanaklata Barua was born on December 22, 1924, in the village of Barangabari, in the Darrang district of Assam. Her life was marked by early tragedy; she lost her mother at the age of five and her father soon after, leaving her to care for her younger siblings. This fostered a deep sense of responsibility that would soon transform into a powerful patriotic resolve.

By 1942, Mahatma Gandhi's call for the Quit India Movement had electrified the nation. The mantra of "Do or Die" resonated deeply in the towns and villages of Assam, including in Kanaklata's district. She was inspired by the speeches of local Congress leaders and, despite her young age, was determined to play her part.

She joined the Mrityu Bahini (Death Squad), a group of youth volunteers formed with the express purpose of sacrificing their lives, if necessary, to uphold the honour of the nation and its flag.

The Fateful Day: September 20, 1942

The local Congress leaders planned a bold act of defiance: to hoist the Tiranga (Indian tricolour flag) at the Gohpur Police Station, a symbol of British authority. This was a direct challenge to the colonial government, which had banned the flag and any such processions.

On September 20, 1942, a large, unarmed procession marched towards the police station, chanting patriotic slogans. At its head, leading the women's contingent with unwavering determination, was 17-year-old Kanaklata Barua, holding the national flag high.

As they reached the station, they were confronted by a police force led by the officer-in-charge, Rebati Mahan Som. He warned the procession to halt, threatening to open fire if they advanced.

Kanaklata's voice rang out, undeterred. She declared that they were unarmed, their intentions were peaceful, and they would not retreat. She was there to do her duty and he could do his.

As she stepped forward, the police fired.

Kanaklata was shot in the chest. She fell to the ground, still clutching the flag, which she tried to keep from falling. As the flag began to dip, another young volunteer from the Mrityu Bahini, Mukunda Kakoti, rushed forward to take it from her. He, too, was shot and killed.

Despite the brutal crackdown, the spirit of the volunteers was not broken. Another person, Rampati Rajkhowa, eventually managed to hoist the flag at the police station, fulfilling the mission for which Kanaklata and Mukunda had laid down their lives.

A Legacy Cast in Courage

Kanaklata Barua's martyrdom at the tender age of 17 sent shockwaves through the region and immortalized her as a symbol of pure, unyielding courage. She was not a trained soldier but an ordinary village girl who, when the moment came, displayed a bravery that humbled the might of an empire.

Her sacrifice remains a cornerstone of Assamese and Indian history. Today, her legacy is honoured in numerous ways:

  • ICGS Kanaklata Barua: The Indian Coast Guard commissioned a Fast Patrol Vessel in her name in 2020, carrying her story of bravery onto the high seas.
  • Statues and Memorials: A life-size statue of her at Gohpur, the site of her martyrdom, and the Kanaklata Udyan, a park in Tezpur, serve as constant reminders of her sacrifice.
  • Birbala Title: The title "Birbala" is now synonymous with her name, a title she earned with her blood.

The story of Kanaklata Barua is a powerful reminder that the fight for freedom was not just waged by prominent leaders in distant cities, but also by teenagers in small villages, whose love for their country was absolute and whose courage knew no bounds.
